<p><p>Phenolic moulding compounds can be classified into two main types: Engineering Grade and General Purpose. Engineering Grade phenolic molding compounds are designed for applications requiring high mechanical strength, heat resistance, and dimensional stability. They are commonly used in aerospace, automotive, and electrical industries. On the other hand, General Purpose phenolic molding compounds are more versatile and cost-effective, suitable for a wide range of applications such as consumer goods, home appliances, and construction materials. Both types offer excellent insulation properties and can be easily molded into various shapes.</p></p>
