<p><p>Epitaxy deposition refers to the process of growing a thin layer of crystal material on a substrate, forming a crystalline film with specific properties. There are various market types within epitaxy deposition, including Metal Organic Chemical Vapor Deposition (MOCVD), Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE), and other types of Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD). MOCVD uses gaseous precursors to deposit layers of thin films, while MBE involves the deposition of atoms or molecules directly onto the substrate. Other CVD epitaxy methods use different chemical reactions to achieve thin film growth. Each of these market types caters to specific applications and industries.</p></p>
