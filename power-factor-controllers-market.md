<p><p>Power Factor Controllers are devices used to improve the power factor of electrical systems. Active Power Factor Controllers are advanced controllers that actively monitor and adjust the power factor in real-time to maintain it at the desired level. They can dynamically correct various loads and provide greater control and efficiency. Passive Power Factor Controllers, on the other hand, are simpler controllers that use fixed components like capacitors or inductors to correct the power factor by offsetting reactive power. They are less flexible and may not provide optimal power factor correction in varying load conditions.</p></p>
