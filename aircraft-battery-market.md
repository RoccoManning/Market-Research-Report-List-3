<p><p>An aircraft battery is a rechargeable energy storage device that provides electrical power to aircraft systems when the engines are not running or in the event of an engine failure. These batteries serve as a critical power source for starting the aircraft engines and ensuring uninterrupted power supply for critical functions like emergency lighting, avionics, and navigation systems.</p><p>The global aircraft battery market has witnessed significant growth over the years due to the increasing demand for aircraft and the growing focus on electric aviation. Factors such as the rising adoption of electric aircraft, advancements in battery technology, and the need for lightweight and high-performance batteries to enhance aircraft efficiency are driving the market growth.</p><p>In addition, the increasing implementation of stringent environmental regulations and policies to reduce carbon emissions is also propelling the demand for electric aircraft batteries. The aerospace industry is increasingly shifting towards electric aviation to achieve sustainable operations, thus boosting the market growth.</p><p>The market is witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth trajectory. One of the prominent trends is the development of lithium-ion batteries for aircraft. Lithium-ion batteries offer higher energy density, longer cycle life, and lower maintenance requirements compared to traditional nickel-cadmium batteries. These batteries provide improved performance and are gaining popularity in the aircraft industry.</p><p>Another trend is the integration of battery management systems (BMS) in aircraft batteries. BMS ensures the safe and effective operation of batteries by monitoring factors such as temperature, voltage, and current. The integration of BMS enhances battery performance, extends battery life, and improves overall aircraft safety.</p><p>Overall, the aircraft battery market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years. According to the provided information, the market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12.2% during the forecast period.</p></p>
