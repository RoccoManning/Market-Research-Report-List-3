<p><p>The recycled metals market is divided into two main categories: ferrous and non-ferrous metals. Ferrous metals contain iron and are magnetic, such as steel and iron, while non-ferrous metals do not contain iron and are not magnetic, such as aluminum, copper, and brass. Ferrous metals are commonly recycled from scrap cars, appliances, and construction materials, while non-ferrous metals are often found in electronics, wiring, and plumbing fixtures. Both markets are essential for reducing waste and conserving natural resources.</p></p>
