<p><p>Grid-tied distributed photovoltaic inverters are connected to the main utility grid and allow users to sell excess electricity back to the grid. Grid/hybrid inverters can operate in both grid-tied and off-grid modes, providing flexibility and energy resilience. Off-grid inverters are not connected to the utility grid and use batteries to store and supply electricity. Each market type offers different benefits and is suitable for different scenarios, depending on factors like location, energy needs, and grid connectivity.</p></p>
