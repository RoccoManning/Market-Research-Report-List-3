<p><p>Sideshaft CV joints are commonly categorized into two main types: Outboard Joints and Inboard Joints. Outboard joints are located at the outer end of the sideshaft and are responsible for transferring power from the transmission to the wheel. On the other hand, inboard joints are situated closer to the transmission and are responsible for transmitting power from the sideshaft to the differential. Both types of joints play a crucial role in allowing for smooth power transfer and rotation in vehicles.</p></p>
