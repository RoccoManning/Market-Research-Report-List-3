<p><p>Ultrafast lasers are a type of laser technology that is capable of emitting extremely short pulses of light, typically in the femtosecond or picosecond range. They find applications in various sectors like scientific research, medicine, and industrial manufacturing. There are different types of ultrafast lasers, including titanium-sapphire lasers, diode-pumped lasers, fiber lasers, and mode-locked diode lasers. Titanium-sapphire lasers use a crystal to generate laser light, while diode-pumped lasers use diodes to pump laser materials. Fiber lasers utilize optical fibers for gaining high power, and mode-locked diode lasers produce ultrafast pulses using diode pumping.  Overall, these lasers offer high precision and fast processing capabilities for diverse fields.</p></p>
