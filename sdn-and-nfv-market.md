<p><p>Software-Defined Networking (SDN) and Network Functions Virtualization (NFV) are innovative technologies that are transforming the networking industry. SDN decouples the control plane from the data plane, allowing for centralized control and programmability of network devices. NFV virtualizes network functions such as firewalls and load balancers, running them on standard servers rather than dedicated hardware.</p><p>The SDN and NFV market is experiencing rapid growth, with a projected CAGR of 14.5% during the forecast period. This growth is being driven by the increasing adoption of cloud computing, IoT, and the need for agile and flexible network infrastructure. Organizations are turning to SDN and NFV to reduce costs, improve network efficiency, and accelerate the deployment of new services.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the SDN and NFV market include the rise of software-defined WAN (SD-WAN) solutions, the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning technologies for network optimization, and the expansion of NFV into the mobile network space with initiatives like virtualized Radio Access Network (vRAN). As the demand for scalable, agile, and cost-effective networking solutions continues to grow, SDN and NFV are expected to play a crucial role in shaping the future of network infrastructure.</p></p>
