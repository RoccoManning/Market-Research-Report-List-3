<p><p>Dialysis access and maintenance solutions are vital for patients undergoing hemodialysis. There are three main types of access for dialysis: fistula, graft, and other access points. Fistulas are created by connecting an artery and vein, providing a strong and reliable access point. Grafts are synthetic tubes implanted beneath the skin to create access for dialysis. Other access points may include catheters or ports. Each type of access requires specific maintenance and care to ensure long-term viability and effectiveness for dialysis treatment.</p></p>
