<p><p>Ink rheology modifiers can be categorized into water-based and solvent-based types within the market. Water-based modifiers are designed for inks that are water-based, providing stability, flow control, and viscosity adjustments. Solvent-based modifiers are formulated for inks that are solvent-based, offering similar functionalities but tailored for compatibility with solvent-based inks. Both types are crucial in the ink industry to enhance print quality, ink performance, and overall process efficiency. Choosing the appropriate modifier type depends on the specific ink formulation and desired printing results.</p></p>
