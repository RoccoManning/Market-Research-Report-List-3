<p><p>Serum-free freezing culture media come in two main types: those containing dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO) and those that are DMSO-free. DMSO is a commonly used cryoprotectant that helps preserve the viability of cells during freezing and thawing. However, some researchers prefer DMSO-free media due to concerns about its potential toxic effects on cells. Both types of media are designed to support the successful freezing and storage of cells for research purposes, allowing for long-term preservation without the need for serum supplementation.</p></p>
