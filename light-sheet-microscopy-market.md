<p><p>Light sheet microscopy is a technique used for imaging biological samples with minimal phototoxicity. One type is the Selective Plane Illumination Microscopy (SPIM), which utilizes a thin sheet of light to illuminate a single plane within the sample. Another type is Dual Inverted Selective Plane Illumination Microscopy (DiSPIM), which uses two opposing objectives for improved resolution. Other variations of light sheet microscopy include techniques like Digital Scanned Laser Light Sheet Fluorescence Microscopy (DSLM) and Ultramicroscopy, each with their own unique advantages and capabilities for imaging various biological specimens.</p></p>
