<p><p>A microcentrifuge is a laboratory instrument used for the separation and analysis of small volumes of biological samples. It works by spinning samples at high speeds to separate components based on their size and density. The microcentrifuge is commonly used in molecular biology, biochemistry, and cell biology research, as well as in clinical and diagnostic laboratories.</p><p>The global microcentrifuge market is experiencing significant growth primarily due to the increasing demand for advanced laboratory equipment and the rise in research activities in the life sciences sector. <p><ul><li>Desktop</li><li>Floor-standing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The microcentrifuge market can be divided into two types: desktop and floor-standing. Desktop microcentrifuges are compact and designed to fit on a laboratory bench, offering ease of use and convenience. They are suitable for low to medium volume applications. On the other hand, floor-standing microcentrifuges are larger and more powerful, capable of handling higher volumes and accommodating a wide range of rotors. They are commonly used in research and industrial settings where larger sample volumes or higher centrifugal forces are required.</p></p>
