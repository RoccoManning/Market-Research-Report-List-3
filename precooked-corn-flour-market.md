<p><p>Precooked corn flour, also known as pre-gelatinized corn flour, is a fine powder made from corn kernels that have been precooked and then grounded into a flour-like consistency. This type of flour is commonly used as a thickening agent in a variety of food products.</p><p>The market for precooked corn flour has been witnessing steady growth in recent years. The increasing demand for convenience foods and ready-to-eat products is one of the key factors driving the market growth. It provides a gluten-free option for individuals with gluten intolerance or celiac disease.</p><p>Moreover, the rising consumer awareness about the nutritional benefits of corn flour, such as its high fiber and protein content, has also contributed to the market growth.
