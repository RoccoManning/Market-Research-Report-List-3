<p><p>Screws for bone fixation are used in orthopedic surgeries to secure fractured bones. The market for these screws is divided into three main types: stainless steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable screws. Stainless steel screws are known for their strength and durability, while titanium screws are lightweight and biocompatible. Bioabsorbable screws eventually dissolve in the body, eliminating the need for a second surgery to remove them. Each type of screw offers unique benefits and is chosen based on the specific needs of the patient and the surgical procedure.</p></p>
