<p><p>Chilled beams are a type of HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) system used in buildings for cooling purposes. There are two market types for chilled beams: bare type and mosaic. </p><p>The bare type chilled beam consists of a cooling coil enclosed in a casing without any additional components. It relies solely on natural convection to circulate the cooled air into the room. On the other hand, the mosaic chilled beam incorporates additional features like heating coils, sound attenuation materials, and diffusers to provide both heating and cooling functions, while maintaining a comfortable environment with reduced noise levels.</p></p>
