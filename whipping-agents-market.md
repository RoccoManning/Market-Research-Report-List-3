<p><p>Whipping agents are substances used in the food industry to stabilize and enhance the texture of whipped products like creams, mousse, and desserts. There are two main types of whipping agents: nonionic and ionic. Nonionic whipping agents, such as lecithin and sorbitan esters, do not carry an electric charge and work by reducing surface tension, resulting in stable foam formation. Ionic whipping agents, such as sodium alginate and potassium caseinate, carry an electrical charge and interact with other ingredients to create stable foam structures. Both types play a crucial role in improving the texture and stability of whipped products.</p></p>
