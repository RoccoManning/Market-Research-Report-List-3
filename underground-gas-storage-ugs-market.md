<p><p>Underground Gas Storage (UGS) includes three market types: depleted fields, aquifers, and salt caverns. Depleted fields refer to depleted oil or gas reservoirs that are repurposed for gas storage. Aquifers are underground water-bearing formations that can store gas due to their porous nature. Salt caverns are created by dissolving salt deposits, forming large underground chambers that can hold gas. These UGS market types offer different advantages and disadvantages in terms of storage capacity, injection rates, withdrawal rates, and cost-efficiency, enabling flexibility and reliability in meeting natural gas demand fluctuations.</p></p>
